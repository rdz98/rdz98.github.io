## About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher jointly affiliated with Zhejiang University and NANHU Brain-Computer Interface Institute. My long-term research vision is to understand the computational principles of the brain and translate them into the next generation of intelligent brain-computer interfaces and biologically inspired artificial intelligence. My research combines artificial intelligence, computational neuroscience, and large-scale neural recording to study how populations of neurons represent sensory information and motor intentions. Rather than treating neural decoding as a purely engineering problem, I aim to develop computational models that are both highly predictive and mechanistically interpretable, enabling us to uncover the neural computations underlying perception and behavior. I am always excited to collaborate with researchers and students who share these interests. If our research directions align, I would be delighted to connect.

## Research Interests

- **Computational Neuroscience:** brain-computer interfaces, visual encoding
- **Trustworthy AI:** privacy-preserving computation, backdoor attacks
